# Warehouse Receiving Standard Operating Procedure

**Document ID:** SCM-SOP-010
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Owner:** Director, Warehouse Operations
**Applies To:** All receiving docks at Contoso distribution centers

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for receiving inbound shipments at Contoso warehouses to ensure accurate inventory, quality compliance, and timely processing.

## 2. Pre-Arrival

1. Verify purchase order status in the procurement system
2. Confirm expected delivery date and carrier information
3. Allocate receiving dock and staging area
4. Prepare receiving documentation (PO copy, inspection checklist)

## 3. Arrival and Unloading

1. Verify carrier and shipment documentation against PO
2. Inspect packaging for visible damage before unloading
3. Count all units/pallets and compare against packing list
4. Note any discrepancies on the receiving report

## 4. Inspection

### 4.1 Standard Inspection
- Visual inspection of all units
- Dimensional check on 10% sample
- Certificate of conformance verification
- Match lot/batch numbers to documentation

### 4.2 Enhanced Inspection (Required for)
- New supplier first shipment: **100% inspection**
- Supplier under quality escalation: **100% inspection**
- Alternate supplier initial orders: **100% inspection for first 3 shipments**
- High-value items (>$500/unit): **25% sample inspection**

## 5. Inventory Update

1. Update inventory system within **4 hours** of receipt completion
2. Record actual received quantities
3. Update days-of-supply calculation
4. Flag any items that bring inventory above max capacity

## 6. Discrepancy Handling

### 6.1 Quantity Discrepancy
- Shortages > 5%: Notify procurement within 2 hours; file carrier claim
- Overages: Receive and notify procurement for PO adjustment

### 6.2 Quality Rejection
- Segregate rejected material in quarantine area
- Notify supplier and procurement within 4 hours
- Generate non-conformance report (NCR)
- If rejection causes stockout risk: trigger expedited shipping per SCM-POL-004

## 7. Late Arrival Processing

When a shipment arrives past the expected delivery date:
1. Record actual arrival date and calculate delay days
2. If delay > 5 days: create incident record
3. If delay > 10 days: notify Supply Chain Manager for penalty assessment per contract terms
4. Update supplier performance metrics automatically
