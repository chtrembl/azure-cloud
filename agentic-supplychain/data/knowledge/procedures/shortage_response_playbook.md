# Shortage Response Playbook

**Document ID:** SCM-PLAY-003
**Effective Date:** February 15, 2024
**Owner:** VP Supply Chain Operations
**Classification:** Internal Operations – Critical Response

---

## 1. Purpose

This playbook provides a structured decision framework for responding to material shortages and stockout risks across the supply chain.

## 2. Shortage Severity Classification

| Level | Days of Supply | Response Time | Decision Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Watch** | 5–10 days | 48 hours | Supply Chain Analyst |
| **Warning** | 3–5 days | 24 hours | Supply Chain Manager |
| **Critical** | < 3 days | Immediate | Director, Supply Chain Ops |
| **Stockout** | 0 days | Immediate | VP Supply Chain |

## 3. Response Procedures

### 3.1 Watch Level (5–10 Days of Supply)

1. Verify open purchase orders and expected delivery dates
2. Contact supplier to confirm shipment status
3. Review demand forecast for next 14 days
4. Prepare expedited shipping request if standard replenishment insufficient
5. Update status daily

### 3.2 Warning Level (3–5 Days of Supply)

1. All Watch-level actions plus:
2. Notify warehouse manager and production planning
3. Submit expedited shipping request per SCM-POL-004 (Manager approval)
4. Contact alternate suppliers on AVL for availability check
5. Assess customer order impact and notify Sales if commitments at risk
6. Update status twice daily

### 3.3 Critical Level (< 3 Days of Supply)

1. All Warning-level actions plus:
2. **Escalate immediately to Director, Supply Chain Operations**
3. Authorize expedited/air freight shipping (Director approval per SCM-POL-004 Section 2.3)
4. Activate emergency qualification of alternate supplier if on AVL (48-hour process)
5. Implement demand rationing if multiple customers affected
6. Initiate supplier escalation per Tier 2/3 of SCM-RUN-001 if delay is supplier-caused
7. Hourly status updates to stakeholders

### 3.4 Stockout (0 Days of Supply)

1. All Critical-level actions plus:
2. **Immediate VP Supply Chain notification**
3. Production line impact assessment within 2 hours
4. Customer communication plan within 4 hours
5. Emergency procurement authorization (VP approval, any source)
6. Post-incident review scheduled within 5 business days

## 4. Cross-Functional Notification Matrix

| Shortage Level | Procurement | Warehouse | Production | Sales | Finance | Executive |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Watch | ✅ | ✅ | | | | |
| Warning | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | | |
| Critical | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | Director |
| Stockout | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | VP + SVP |

## 5. Recovery and Post-Incident

1. Document root cause and contributing factors
2. Update supplier performance record
3. Assess financial impact (expedited costs, penalties, lost revenue)
4. Revise safety stock levels if systemic issue identified
5. Update this playbook if gaps identified
