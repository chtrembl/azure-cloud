# Supplier Performance Escalation Runbook

**Document ID:** SCM-RUN-001
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2024
**Owner:** Director, Supplier Management
**Classification:** Internal Operations

---

## 1. Purpose

This runbook defines the escalation process when a supplier's performance degrades below acceptable thresholds.

## 2. Escalation Tiers

### Tier 1 – Performance Advisory (On-Time Rate 70%–89%)

**Trigger:** Supplier on-time delivery rate falls below 90% in any rolling 30-day period.

**Actions:**
1. Supply Chain Analyst generates performance report
2. Account Manager sends formal advisory letter within 5 business days
3. Schedule performance review meeting within 10 business days
4. Document corrective actions agreed upon
5. Set 30-day checkpoint for improvement

### Tier 2 – Formal Escalation (On-Time Rate 50%–69%)

**Trigger:** Supplier on-time delivery rate falls below 70%, OR Tier 1 improvement not achieved within 30 days.

**Actions:**
1. Supply Chain Manager issues formal written notice
2. Require supplier corrective action plan within **10 business days**
3. Initiate alternate supplier qualification in parallel
4. Weekly performance tracking calls
5. Escalate to Director, Supplier Management for oversight

### Tier 3 – Critical Escalation (On-Time Rate < 50%)

**Trigger:** Supplier on-time delivery rate falls below **50% in any rolling 90-day period**.

**Actions:**
1. **Immediate notification** to VP Supply Chain
2. Mandatory escalation meeting within **5 business days**
3. Activate dual-sourcing with qualified alternate suppliers
4. Issue formal cure notice under contract terms
5. Begin termination assessment if no improvement within 30 days
6. Daily shipment tracking and expedited shipping authorization

## 3. Decision Authority

| Tier | Decision Maker | Can Authorize |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Supply Chain Manager | Advisory notices, review meetings |
| Tier 2 | Director, Supplier Management | Formal escalation, alternate qualification |
| Tier 3 | VP Supply Chain | Dual-sourcing, contract termination, emergency procurement |

## 4. Documentation

All escalation actions must be documented in the Supplier Performance Management system, including:
- Date and trigger event
- Actions taken and timelines
- Supplier response and corrective action plan
- Resolution or further escalation decision
