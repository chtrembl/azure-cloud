# Expedited Shipping Authorization Policy

**Policy Number:** SCM-POL-004
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Owner:** VP Supply Chain Operations
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes authorization levels and procedures for expedited shipping to maintain continuity of supply while controlling transportation costs.

## 2. Authorization Levels

### 2.1 Standard Expedited (≤ $5,000 shipping cost)
- **Authorized by:** Supply Chain Manager
- **Approval method:** Email or procurement system approval
- **Turnaround:** Same business day

### 2.2 Premium Expedited ($5,001 – $25,000 shipping cost)
- **Authorized by:** Director, Supply Chain Operations
- **Approval method:** Formal written approval with business justification
- **Turnaround:** Within 4 business hours

### 2.3 Emergency Air Freight (> $25,000 or critical stockout)
- **Authorized by:** VP Supply Chain or Operations Director
- **Approval method:** Emergency authorization form + verbal confirmation
- **Turnaround:** Immediate
- **Note:** For critical stockout situations (days of supply < 3), the Operations Director may authorize air freight at **up to 3x standard shipping cost**

## 3. Triggering Conditions

Expedited shipping is justified when:
- Days of supply falls below **reorder point** and standard replenishment will not arrive in time
- Customer delivery commitments are at risk
- Production line stoppage is imminent
- Quality rejection of incoming shipment requires emergency replacement

## 4. Documentation Requirements

All expedited shipping authorizations must include:
1. Purchase order reference
2. Product and quantity
3. Standard vs. expedited cost comparison
4. Business impact justification
5. Approver name and date

## 5. Cost Allocation

- Expedited shipping costs attributed to **root cause** cost center
- If caused by supplier late delivery: charge back to supplier account where contract permits
- Monthly reporting of expedited shipping spend to Finance and VP Supply Chain

## 6. Carrier Selection

- Use contracted carriers (NorthStar Logistics for Northeast) where possible
- Spot market carriers require procurement team approval
- Air freight carriers must be on the approved carrier list
